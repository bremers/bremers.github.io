My PhD dissertation at Cornell, ["Towards Mixed-Initiative Machines for Creative Tasks,"](https://drive.google.com/file/d/15xxAHymRhzXQOVyNlroFgNUOu0U45X7s/view?usp=sharing) investigates how interactive and intelligent machines can support human workflows during physical tasks, especially fabrication. I integrate human-computer interaction, design, and systems development, working with collaborators across academia and industry.

Before Cornell, I spent three years as an HMI Researcher at Jaguar Land Rover, collaborating with Cambridge University on AR head-up displays and driver workload modeling. During my PhD, I interned at Walt Disney Imagineering (creativity support tools), Accenture Labs (task guidance systems), and Toyota Research Institute (computer vision for street-level data).

My background spans Industrial Design (B.S., TU Eindhoven), Artificial Intelligence (M.S., Utrecht), and Information Science (Ph.D., Cornell), with a CS minor in computer graphics. I'm a Cornell PiTech AI in Arts & Culture Fellow.
