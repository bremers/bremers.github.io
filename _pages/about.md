** Human-AI/Robot Interaction, UX/HF, Engineer, Creative Technologist. Open to work from July 2026 onwards! Looking for my next R&D role in AI and robotics with a strong human-focused component. NY/CA/MA preferred, but open to relocation. Authorized to work in the US.**

I’m a [PhD Graduate](https://infosci.cornell.edu/people/alexandra-bremers) in Information Science at Cornell University (thesis defended April 2026), based at Cornell Tech in New York City. My research, advised by [Wendy Ju](https://wendyju.com), addresses how interactive and intelligent machines can support human workflows during physical tasks, especially fabrication. My doctoral work integrated human–computer interaction, design, and systems development, and I am a Cornell PiTech AI in Arts & Culture Fellow. (You can read my thesis [here!](https://drive.google.com/file/d/15xxAHymRhzXQOVyNlroFgNUOu0U45X7s/view?usp=sharing))

I am a designer at heart, with a strong research and technical training that allows me to not only envision, but also prototype, build, and test new ways for people to achieve things in the world. Aside from 6 years as a PhD researcher, I bring 3 years of full-time industry research experience. Prior to Cornell, I worked as a Human–Machine Interface Researcher at Jaguar Land Rover in the UK, where I collaborated with the University of Cambridge and was recognized through the Oxford–Cambridge RisingWISE program and JLR & Tata Innovista innovation awards. This experience was expanded with internships at Walt Disney Imagineering R&D, Accenture Labs, and Toyota Research Institute during my PhD studies.

My academic background spans Information Science (M.S., Cornell), Artificial Intelligence (M.S., Utrecht University), and Industrial Design (B.S., TU Eindhoven, see [my portfolio](https://bremers.github.io/cv/Bremers_portfolio2025.pdf)). In 2020, I was awarded the Royal Commission for the Exhibition of 1851 Industrial Fellowship to pursue doctoral study at Cambridge.
