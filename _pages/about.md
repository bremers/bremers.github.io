I am currently a 4th year PhD Candidate in the Information Science department at Cornell University, based at Cornell Tech (New York, NY). I am advised by associate professor [Wendy Ju](https://wendyju.com), with professor [Francois Guimbretiere](https://www.cs.cornell.edu/~francois/) and professor [Steve Marschner](https://www.cs.cornell.edu/~srm/) on my committee.

My research focuses on human-robot collaboration for physical manufacturing and fabrication, leveraging AI and human capabilities. I am specifically interested in how machines can support the human experience of making. The methods that I use focus most on interaction design, interactive device prototyping and video analysis. You can find my cv [here](https://bremers.github.io/cv/Bremers_cv.pdf), my resume [here](https://bremers.github.io/cv/Bremers_resume.pdf), and my papers on [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=WB5PtlIAAAAJ&hl=en) or [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Alexandra-Bremers).

I hold a master's degree in Artificial Intelligence from Utrecht University (M.S.), and a bachelor's degree in Industrial Design from Eindhoven University of Technology (B.S.). Between 2017 and 2020 I was employed as Human-Machine Interface Researcher at Jaguar Land Rover Automotive PLC, based at the National Automotive Innovation Centre in Coventry, United Kingdom. Other work experiences include Toyota Research Institute and Accenture Labs R&D.

My work has been published in top venues in human-computer interaction and human-robot interaction, including IJHCS and IROS, and I co-authored work shown at CHI, SCF, HRI, TEI, AutoUI and IEEE IV.
