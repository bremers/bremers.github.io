I am a PhD Candidate in Information Science at Cornell University, based at Cornell Tech in New York, NY. I work with [Wendy Ju](https://wendyju.com), [Francois Guimbretiere](https://www.cs.cornell.edu/~francois/) and [Steve Marschner](https://www.cs.cornell.edu/~srm/) on interaction design for multi-modal, physical tasks. 

I hold an M.S. in Information Science from Cornell University, an M.S. in Artificial Intelligence from Utrecht University, and a B.S. in Industrial Design from Eindhoven University of Technology. Additionally, I studied automotive human-technology interaction at Warwick University. From 2017 to 2020, I worked as a Human-Machine Interface Researcher at Jaguar Land Rover, UK, on collaborative research with Cambridge University. While at Jaguar, I was selected to join the 2018 Oxford-Cambridge cohort of Rising Women in Science and Engineering (RisingWISE), and was recognized for innovation through the JLR & Tata Innovista competitions. I also spent time at Toyota Research Institute and Accenture Labs R&D in the Bay Area, and the NTU IoX Center at National Taiwan University.

In addition to my technical work, I have been involved in art and design projects with Next Nature Network and Dutch Design Week, and won awards in creative and print writing. In 2020, I received the Royal Commission of 1851 Industrial Fellowship to pursue a PhD in Engineering at the University of Cambridge.

My work has been published at ACM/IEEE conferences such as IROS, CHI, HRI, TEI, AutoUI, SCF, CUI, HTTF and RO-MAN, as well as in the journals IEEE IV and Elsevier IJHCS.

My cv can be found [here](https://bremers.github.io/cv/Bremers_cv.pdf), my resume [here](https://bremers.github.io/cv/Bremers_resume.pdf), and my papers on [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=WB5PtlIAAAAJ&hl=en) or [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Alexandra-Bremers).
