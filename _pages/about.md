I am a designer and researcher from The Netherlands, residing in the United Kingdom. In day to day work I design and develop interactive, artificially intelligent systems for use inside the car cabin. My wider research interests focus around "everyday interactions with intelligent cyber-physical systems for non-expert users" (I will explain this in more detail). I obtained degrees in Industrial Design from Eindhoven University of Technology, and Artificial Intelligence from Utrecht University, both in The Netherlands. On this personal website I showcase my public work. You can also find my CV, recent news, as well as links to my other social media accounts. This website is currently a work in progress - more materials will be added along the way. The website works best on a pc or laptop. 

My interest in design originates from a fascination with the objects that surround us in everyday life. Everything man-made was, at some point, invented and created by someone (or most likely by a number of people), to act as a tool for a certain purpose. Ranging from cutlery, to clothing, furniture, and more recently, smartphones, home assistants - all these are displays of human ingenuity, that we own, touch, take into our hands and experience closely throughout our lives.   

Where we have a lot of tools that can do something of a physical or mechanical nature, that the humans can't do (or are unwilling to do), there is also a category of tools that can do something of an intellectual nature - in a sense, human ingenuity has led to us developing tools that can help us in our ingenuity. This is the category of tools that I find most interesting: I call them intelligent cyber-physical systems (because they usually have both a virtual and physical presence).
