About

My PhD dissertation at Cornell, Towards Mixed-Initiative Machines for Creative Tasks, investigates how interactive and intelligent machines can support human workflows during physical tasks, especially fabrication. I integrate human-computer interaction, design, and systems development, working with collaborators across academia and industry.

Before Cornell, I spent three years as an HMI Researcher at Jaguar Land Rover, collaborating with Cambridge University on AR head-up displays and driver workload modeling. During my PhD, I interned at Walt Disney Imagineering (creativity support tools), Accenture Labs (task guidance systems), and Toyota Research Institute (computer vision).

My background spans Industrial Design (B.S., TU Eindhoven), Artificial Intelligence (M.S., Utrecht), and Information Science (Ph.D., Cornell), with a CS minor in computer graphics. I'm a Cornell PiTech AI in Arts & Culture Fellow.

Creative Machines2026

Proactive Sewing Assistance (CoSew-4)

A Wizard-of-Oz study comparing proactive and reactive machine assistance during a sewing task with 20 novice participants. Proactive assistance, where the machine anticipated confusion based on behavioral cues, was perceived as significantly more timely without increasing annoyance. Resulted in the CoSew-4 synchronized multi-view video dataset.
